About The Party

The Indian National Congress is a political party that has played a pivotal role in shaping India's history and future. Founded in 1885, it is one of the oldest and most prominent political parties in India. From its inception, the Indian National Congress has been at the forefront of the country's freedom struggle against colonial rule. It was instrumental in leading India to independence from the British Empire in 1947, and since then, it has played a vital role in shaping India's socio-economic and political landscape. The Indian National Congress has a rich legacy of leadership, including iconic figures such as Mahatma Gandhi, Jawaharlal Nehru, and Indira Gandhi. These leaders have not only contributed significantly to India's independence but have also been instrumental in shaping the country's vision for a modern and prosperous future. The Congress has also been a champion of India's pluralistic society and secular values. It has been at the forefront of advocating for the rights of marginalized communities, promoting social justice, and striving towards equality for all. In recent times, the Indian National Congress has undergone significant changes and reorganizations to remain relevant and responsive to the changing political landscape. It has adopted new policies and strategies to address the emerging challenges faced by the country and its people. Today, the Indian National Congress remains a vital political force in India, with a committed base of supporters and leaders who continue to strive for a better and brighter future for the country. Its legacy of service, sacrifice, and leadership has inspired generations of Indians to contribute to the country's development and progress. In conclusion, the Indian National Congress is a storied political party that has left an indelible mark on India's history and continues to play a vital role in shaping the country's future. Its commitment to the values of democracy, secularism, and social justice is an inspiration to all those who seek to build a better world.

Our Philosophy

The philosophy of the Indian National Congress is based on the principles of democracy, secularism, and socialism. It seeks to uphold the values of equality, social justice, and human rights, and aims to create a society that is inclusive and equitable. The Congress believes in a decentralized political system that promotes local self-government and empowers people at the grassroots level. It also advocates for the protection of the rights and freedoms of individuals, and seeks to ensure that all citizens have access to basic necessities such as education, healthcare, and employment opportunities. The party's philosophy is deeply rooted in India's history and cultural heritage, and it draws inspiration from the country's struggle for independence and the teachings of great leaders like Mahatma Gandhi and Jawaharlal Nehru. The Congress seeks to promote harmony and unity among diverse communities in India, and works towards building a just and peaceful society for all.

History of party

The Indian National Congress (INC) is an iconic political party that has shaped the course of Indian history. Established in 1885, the Congress played a vital role in India's fight for independence and produced some of the country's greatest leaders. From Mahatma Gandhi's non-violent struggle for freedom to Jawaharlal Nehru's vision of a socialist India, the Congress has left an indelible mark on India's political and social landscape. The party has faced its fair share of challenges over the years, but its commitment to democracy, secularism, and social justice has remained unwavering. Even after India gained independence, the Congress continued to lead the country and played a crucial role in shaping its future. The party's progressive policies paved the way for land reforms, the expansion of public education, and the growth of a socialist economy. Today, the Indian National Congress continues to inspire millions of people across India and around the world. Its legacy of promoting inclusivity, justice, and equality remains relevant in the modern world and continues to shape the political discourse in India.
